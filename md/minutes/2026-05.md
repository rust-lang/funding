*Connecting maintainers and money*

Our goal is to help people get funded to work on Rust.

## Rust Foundation Maintainer Fund RFC

The Rust Foundation Maintainer Fund RFC established two things. The first one is how the RFMF money is allocated.

* *Rust Foundation Maintainer Fund* -- money that is made available to the leadership council but which is reserved for uses that "primarily fund maintainers"

In [rust-lang/leadership-council#294](
https://github.com/rust-lang/leadership-council/issues/294), Leadership council then established the *funding team* to deterine how to use the funding that comes in:

![rust-lang/leadership-council#294](https://hedgedoc.nikomatsakis.com/uploads/52b2da95-8000-4abc-b996-4064ce768d90.png)

## Let's get real

* We have some money lined up
* We're going to start with some concrete things, fund some folks, get some outcomes
* This is going to help us get more

## Sources of funding

* Individual and small contributions
    * Foundation github sponsors
* Companies and larger donors
    * Run the gamut in terms of how involved they want to be
    * Mostly driven by internal Rust advocates:
        * Not *that* picky about how money is used
        * *But* do want to influence selection to ensure that they'll be able to make the case for more funding next year
        * Happy to have some $$ go to "lesser known" things, as long as there's enough "red meat" to keep everyone happy

## Programs

### Funding needs 

Step 1 is gathering up a list of

* Which maintainers are interested in funding
* What areas would they like to work on, details of the proposal
* Funding details (how much funding are they looking for, what kind of work structures are suitable, etc)

Presumably the last one would be kept private (how private).

### Project goals blocked on funding

Add "Funding" section to project goals template. Leverage new, lighter-weight process.

Funding section identifies

* What funding is required to fund *contribution*
* What funding is required to fund *review*

Role of the funding team: liase with team leads to determine whether the review bandwidth for "goals in need of funding" is adequate and how to resolve.

Publish and advertise funding needs.

Use some of the RFMF funds to cover project goals.

### Maintainer in Residence

Also established by the RFMF RFC. 
The RFC also outlined the formation of a *Maintainer in Residence* program

* Long-term contracts under the Rust Foundation with *expectation* of renewal
* Grantees apply with a general description of what they would like to do
    * e.g., contract a "maintainer in residence" for the types team, for rustfmt, for rustdoc, etc

The MiR program (long-term, renewed contracts under the Foundation) funds individuals to do undirected work in a particular area. Contract with the Foundation.

Intended to be light touch. Ideally full time though we did not require that to ensure perfect is not enemy of good (e.g., may be useful to have somebody who does 50% MiR in the Rust project and 50% time on other things, particularly as the program is bootstrpaping and available funding is limited).
